Ithikkara Pakki

Ithikkara Pakki was an Indian outlaw who lived in the Travancore Kingdom in the 19th century. He is said to have plundered from the rich and distributed to the poor. He is said to have moved as swiftly as a bird or butterfly, meaning Pakki in Malayalam, hence he was known by the name Ithikkara Pakki. Legends on him are part of the folklore of Kerala where he is represented as a heroic outlaw. Stories about him often include his friend and fellow bandit Kayamkulam Kochunni.

Thacholi Chandu

Thacholi Chandu was a warrior and a legendary hero who is believed to have lived during 17th century in North Malabar region of Kerala, India. He belonged to the Thacholi family. He is praised about in Vadakkan Pattukal (ballads of North Malabar). He is known for conquering the Thulunadan fort which is a feat even his legendary uncle Thacholi Othenan couldn't accomplish.
